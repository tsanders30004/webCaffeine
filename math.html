The Z-Transform

The Z-transform of a sequence $x[n]$ is defined by the formula $$X(z) =\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}x[n]z^{-n}.$$

Consider the following:

$$\begin{matrix}x[n] & = & 3 \left ( \frac{1}{2}\right )^{n}+2\left (-\frac{1}{3}\right )^{n}+6\delta[n] \\ & = & \begin{cases} 11, & n=0\\ 3 \left ( \frac{1}{2}\right )^{n}+2\left (-\frac{1}{3}\right )^{n}, & n>0\end{cases}\end{matrix}$$we therefore can conclude that $$X(z) = \frac{11-z^{-1}-z^{-2}}{1-\frac{1}{6}z^{-1}-\frac{1}{6}z^{-2}} =\frac{3}{1-\frac{1}{2}z^{-1}} + \frac{2}{1+\frac{1}{3}z^{-1}}+6$$

The Fibonacci Sequence

The Fibonacci Sequence is defined as $f[n] = {0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, ...}$. Each element of the sequence is the sum of the preceding two elements.

The sequence appears surprisingly often in higher mathematics - and in nature. The following naturally occurring things are related to the Fibonacci Sequence:

  • Branching in trees
  • Arragement of stems on a pineappe
  • Arragement of bracts on a pine cone

Of particular interest to the author is the relationship of this sequence to the so-called Fibonacci Spiral, the shape of which occurs frequenly in architecure. A curious characteristic of this sequence is that it appears so regularly in the sciences, and in artforms.

Using mathematical notation commonly observed in signal processing, the sequence can be characterized with the following formula: $$f[n] = \frac{\varphi^n-(-\varphi)^{-n}}{2\varphi -1}$$

Extending the signal processing metaphor, it is possible to derive a closed form expression of the Fibonacci Sequence by applying the $Z$-Transform of a signal $f[n]$:

$$F(z) =\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}f[n]z^{-n}$$ The forward shift propery of the $Z$-Transform stipulates that the $Z$-Transform of $f[n+1]$ is equal to $z(F(z)-f[0])$. By applying this property to the Fibonacci Sequence $f[n]$ shows, after a lot of messy algebra, that the $n{th}$ element of the sequence can be non-recursively calculated as $f[n] = \frac{\varphi^n-(-\varphi)^{-n}}{2\varphi -1}$ where $\varphi=\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}$.
